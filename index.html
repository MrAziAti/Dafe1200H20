Salaries are not just about the main skills

Usually, secondary skills are wanted as well. And experience counts a lot.

Therefore, please mint that "median salary" is not first-job pay(not starting positions). So with a lot of experience Java-programmer out there, the median salary may be muvh higher than starting salary.

Concering secondary skills, look at "GO". A new language, compared to Java, with a tenth of the "Historical permanent job ads" of java. It is better paid in general, which shoud leed us to assume that it may have a much higher starting salary. That would not be odd. Go is used for scalebility in the networked servers. If you know Go, you probably now a bit about scaleable network servers as well.

The difficulty of comparing salaries

The difficulty in comparing salaries, is easiest illustrated by looking at men versus women.

Men consistently earn more than woman in general. However, whenever we attempt to brake it down and ensure that we compared similar jobs with comperable task, the difference almost disapperes. This was illustradet yet again, in a recent discussion about pay in academia

Her ser vi eit interessant mønster: mennene tener i gjennomsnitt nesten 65 tusen meir enn kvinnene, trass i at kvinner tener meir enn menn i tre av dei fire stillingskodane. Det er kun for førsteamanuensar at mennene tener mest. Ein kan også legge merke til at lønnsforskjellane for kvar stillingskode er små (mellom to og åtte tusen) samanlikna med den aggregerte forskjellen på 65 tusen. Den store forskjellen mellom menns og kvinners lønn kjem altså av at dei har ulik jobb. Den vanlegaste stillinga for mennene i utvalget er professor, medan den vanlegaste stillinga for kvinnene er stipendiat. (From Khrono, Gender and salary statistics, 19/07/20).

Glass doors, ceilings and salary

If we continue to look at women and pay, does this mean that we just have to wait and the pay-gap will sort it self out? Preliminiary data from the IT-businessen is not just promissing. A recent report (from HackerRank) highlight how women consistently work in more junior positions than men at the same age.

The importance of information

In norway, you may ask for the exact salary and pay (with overtime) for anyone publicly employed. You want to know my salary? Ask OsloMet. Webpages like those mentioned aboce, as well as labour unions, try to collect salary data so you can find out how your pay compares to other.

Yet, as we know from studies on women and advancement, about recruitment and people without network -either from out of town , or worse, foreign - who you know may be more important.

That is why unions, and webpages like Glassdor.com are important. They will help you see if you recieve a fair pay. And they may shareinformation about how fait the overall culture is in a company. (Glassdoor.com does, unions may not)
